## Interview Guest

**Xu Rui** — First Response Event Medical Director / Marathon Medical Support Standards Drafting Expert

## Core Insights

Xu Rui has dedicated 10 years to marathon medical support, covering over 300 events of various scales and handling more than 20 cardiac arrest cases along with numerous heat-related emergencies. Through cases like the 2025 Dalian Marathon, he systematically explains the complexity of marathon medical support and how the two standards help build this system.

### Marathon Medical Support is a Complex Systematic Project

Marathon medical support is not simply "arranging doctors and ambulances" — it is a complex systematic project encompassing: project analysis, participant scale assessment, course reconnaissance, emergency vehicle planning, transport route design, personnel training and assessment, equipment provision, and operational organization. Every link is interconnected; a gap in any one can compromise the entire system.

### Every Event Requires a Unique Plan

There is no "one-size-fits-all" solution. Different cities have different medical resource distributions, course configurations, climate conditions, and participant demographics. Preparation begins 1-2 months before race day: on-site course surveys, multiple meetings with health authorities, recruitment → skills training → assessment → drills → race day execution.

### The Dalian Marathon Case Study

The 2025 Dalian Marathon exemplifies standards implementation. Following the requirements of T/CADERM 8001-2021 and T/CADERM 8002-2021, a detailed course risk assessment was conducted, with personnel and AED numbers exceeding baseline requirements. The event successfully managed multiple heat-related incidents with zero cardiac arrest fatalities.

### Commitment and Vision

Since the standards were released, Xu Rui and his team have consistently executed at or above the required levels. He hopes more people will recognize the necessity of industry standards and that the standards will continue to evolve with frontline feedback.
