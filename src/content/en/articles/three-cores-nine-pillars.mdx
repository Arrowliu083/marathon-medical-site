## A Proven Track Record

Guangdong Xinzhong Sports Group, founded in 2014, has operated over 600 large-scale events across 80 cities in 23 provinces, serving more than 7 million runners — making it China's most experienced race operations company. When Xinzhong contributed its operational expertise to the standards drafting process, it brought not theoretical models but battle-tested systems refined through hundreds of real-world events.

## Pre-Race: Prevention and Empowerment

Xinzhong's philosophy is that prevention outperforms treatment. The pre-race phase combines rigorous participant health screening — including qualification review, health questionnaires, and mandatory insurance — with a comprehensive first-aid training ecosystem. In 2018, Xinzhong became the first Chinese race company to establish an American Heart Association (AHA) cardiovascular emergency training center. This center has since certified thousands of first responders, creating a pool of trained "first-aid runners" who participate in races as an additional layer of on-course safety.

## In-Race: The Multi-Tier Response Network

During events, Xinzhong deploys a layered defense system: a medical command center coordinating with designated hospitals, medical stations paired with ambulances every 2.5 km, bicycle-mounted AED responders every kilometer, observation volunteers every 100 meters, and first-aid-trained runners embedded within the participant field. This architecture is designed to guarantee a one-minute response to any incident anywhere on the course. The command structure integrates public security and health authorities in a unified joint command, ensuring seamless coordination between on-course response and hospital admission.

## Results That Speak

In 2025, across more than 100 events operated by Xinzhong, there were 148 injuries requiring hospital transfer — including 9 cases of cardiac arrest. All 9 cardiac arrest victims survived. These results are not accidents; they are the direct product of a standardized, professionally staffed, and continuously rehearsed medical support system. They represent the most compelling evidence that the standards, when implemented with rigor and operational expertise, deliver on their fundamental promise: protecting runners' lives.
