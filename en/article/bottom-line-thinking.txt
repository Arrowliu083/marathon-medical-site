b:Ta00,## From Growth to Governance

Since China deregulated marathon approvals in 2015, the country has witnessed explosive growth in road running events — from just 51 events per year to over 1,000 before 2020. Marathons have evolved from niche athletic competitions into major civic events that showcase city identity, promote public fitness, and drive sports tourism. This rapid expansion, however, has brought safety risks to the forefront. A marathon is not an ordinary public gathering: its long distance, extended duration, high participant density, open course, and wide variation in individual health status create unique and significant medical challenges.

## The Baiyin Catalyst

The 2021 Baiyin cross-country running tragedy, which claimed 21 lives, was a watershed moment for the industry. The official investigation identified not only extreme weather as a factor, but also insufficient operational capacity, inadequate medical resources, and the absence of emergency response plans. In the aftermath, the Chinese Athletics Association and the China Association for Medical Rescue — with support from Tencent Charity — rapidly assembled experts from across the country to draft the two standards T/CADERM 8001-2021 and T/CADERM 8002-2021.

## Seven Core Problems

The standards address a range of systemic failures observed in real-world events: events operating without any medical support at all; medical personnel whose skills do not match marathon-specific needs; insufficient ambulance numbers; using transport-only ambulances as substitutes for fully equipped ones; on-site resources without unified command; poorly defined roles within medical command centers; communication failures that render medical teams ineffective; first responders who lack basic life-support skills; and the absence of standardized medical incident documentation.

## From Experience to Standards

The publication of these two standards marks a fundamental shift in China's marathon industry. Medical support is no longer a matter of individual judgment or budget discretion — it is now a system that can be designed, inspected, trained for, executed, recorded, and reviewed. The standards do not prescribe maximum or ideal configurations; they establish minimum requirements and explicitly call for upward adjustment based on course complexity, weather conditions, and visibility.
