b:T988,## A Seasoned Leader in Emergency Medicine

Dr. Cai Wenwei serves as Director of the Emergency Department at Zhejiang Provincial People's Hospital and Deputy Director of the Zhejiang Provincial Emergency Command Center. Since 2013, he has led Zhejiang's National Emergency Medical Rescue Team in major disaster response operations and directed medical support planning for landmark events including the G20 Hangzhou Summit, the Hangzhou Asian Games, and numerous large-scale sporting events. His perspective on marathon medical standards is grounded in this unusually broad experience.

## The Case for Standardized Protocols

Dr. Cai observes that medical incidents at mass-participation events are extraordinarily diverse — "a thousand strange situations," as he puts it — and that medical personnel who are new to event support lack effective, standardized operating procedures to guide their response. Without clear protocols, even experienced clinicians can struggle in the unfamiliar赛道 environment. The standards fill this gap by distilling expert consensus and best practices into actionable, replicable procedures that any qualified medical professional can follow.

## Standardization + Localization: A Dual Drive

Dr. Cai is emphatic that standards should not be applied mechanically. Every city has a different distribution of medical resources, different traffic patterns, and different climatic conditions. The medical support plan for Shaoxing cannot be identical to the plan for Hangzhou, let alone Beijing or Shanghai. The correct approach is a dual-drive model: the national standards provide the framework and the minimum requirements; local adaptation fills in the details based on actual conditions. The G20 and Asian Games experiences validated this approach — the standards defined what "good" looks like, and local teams determined how to achieve it with their specific resources.

## From Publication to Practice

For the standards to have real impact, Dr. Cai recommends that local health commissions incorporate them as mandatory conditions for event approval, that race organizers specify standards compliance in procurement documents, that supervisory and evaluation mechanisms be established, and that frontline feedback be systematically collected to inform future revisions.
