Interview: Building a Systematic Medical Support Project with Standards

Xu Rui
First Response Event Medical Director / Standards Drafting Expert
Published
2026-06-09

Interview Guest

Xu Rui — First Response Event Medical Director / Marathon Medical Support Standards Drafting Expert

Core Insights

Xu Rui has dedicated 10 years to marathon medical support, covering over 300 events of various scales and handling more than 20 cardiac arrest cases along with numerous heat-related emergencies.

Interview Video

Marathon Medical Support is a Complex Systematic Project

Marathon medical support is not simply "arranging doctors and ambulances" — it is a complex systematic project encompassing: project analysis, participant scale assessment, course reconnaissance, emergency vehicle planning, transport route design, personnel training and assessment, equipment provision, and operational organization.

Every Event Requires a Unique Plan

There is no "one-size-fits-all" solution. Different cities have different medical resource distributions, course configurations, climate conditions, and participant demographics. Preparation begins 1-2 months before race day.

The Dalian Marathon Case Study

The 2025 Dalian Marathon exemplifies standards implementation. Following T/CADERM 8001-2021 and T/CADERM 8002-2021, a detailed course risk assessment was conducted, with personnel and AED numbers exceeding baseline requirements. The event achieved zero cardiac arrest fatalities.

Commitment and Vision

Since the standards were released, Xu Rui and his team have consistently executed at or above the required levels. He hopes more people will recognize the necessity of industry standards and that they will continue to evolve with frontline feedback.
